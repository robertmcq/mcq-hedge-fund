# References and Bibliography

This document maintains a canonical bibliography for the MCQ documentation set.
It separates foundational theory from later implementation details so that readers can
trace which concepts come from established literature and which are MCQ-specific design
choices.

---

## Foundational Theory

- Harry Markowitz (1952) — *Portfolio Selection*
- John L. Kelly Jr. (1956) — *A New Interpretation of Information Rate*
- William F. Sharpe (1964) — *Capital Asset Prices: A Theory of Market Equilibrium under Conditions of Risk*
- Eugene F. Fama (1970) — *Efficient Capital Markets: A Review of Theory and Empirical Work*
- David R. Cox (1972) — *Regression Models and Life-Tables*
- Stephen A. Ross (1976) — *The Arbitrage Theory of Capital Asset Pricing*

---

## Factor Models and Performance Attribution

- Eugene F. Fama and Kenneth R. French (1993) — *Common Risk Factors in the Returns on Stocks and Bonds*
- Mark M. Carhart (1997) — *On Persistence in Mutual Fund Performance*

---

## Behavioural Finance and Market Regimes

- Robert J. Shiller (1981) — *Do Stock Prices Move Too Much to Be Justified by Subsequent Changes in Dividends?*
- Robert J. Shiller (2015) — *Irrational Exuberance* / later narrative-economics work

---

## Implementation Note

The following are **MCQ implementation concepts**, not terms taken from the academic
literature:

- Governance Score
- Enforcement Loss (EnfLoss)
- MCQ Governance Covariate Model
- Velocity, Volume, Shadow covariates
- Five-panel architecture and governance queue

These should always be presented as internal design constructs rather than attributed to
academic or university course sources.
